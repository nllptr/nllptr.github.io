So you found me. Great! If you’re reading this I’m going to aassume that you want to know more about me and who I am.

First of all, my name is Simon. I live in Hässleholm in the southern part of Sweden. I live here with my wife and two kids. Our family also includes cats and chickens. We really love spending our time in the garden, trying to grow our own food as best we can. We are still new at it, but the only way to get better at that stuff is getting your hands dirty and learn while doing.

To support this lifestyle of ours, I work as a Java developer at Tyringekonsult AB in the town of Tyringe, just 20 minutes away. Tyringekonsult is really good at EDI and sells services for logistics, automotive, trade, and most recently, the build industry. At work, I live mostly in Java land and do web development with the Apache Wicket framework. There is a lot of SQL involved, so I consider myself somewhat proficient with that as well.

However, this site is about the Go programming language. I’ve gotten really hooked by this language. Here are some reasons I like it:

  1. It’s small. There isn’t really that much to learn in terms of keywords and language constructs. Usually there’s only one way to do them and that’s good.

  2. It has a great standard library! I like Python for the same reason.

  3. The tools that come with the language are awesome.

  4. I can cross compile without worries like a boss.

  5. It has the best mascot ever.
